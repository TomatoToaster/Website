Spilly Goose up till now

Prototype to 1.0.8

Spilly Goose is a project that stemmed from an idea that my friend gave me. That idea became a prototype where you play as a goose who's trying to balance a stack of coffee cups on its head, while avoiding obstacles, for as long as you can. You could grow your stack, which gets you more points per second, but to do so, you have to line your stack up with the coffee of walking pedestrians.

I tested it with folks I knew in real life, and the feedback was that it was difficult, but really fun/satisfying when the balancing act "clicked". Some of us got so into it, we were chasing each other's high scores. I truly learned the meaning of "beaten at my own game". The stack-balance-dodge gameplay was a new sensation/method of play, which was novel, but frustrating if you didn't get it quickly. I identified that the game needed to be easier in the beginning, or maybe teach the players how to play it progressively.

The original goal was to have it be an arcade style endless runner with the stack-balance-dodge gameplay. The learning curve posed a challenge to that. I didn't want to make the game too much easier, because the most fun I've recorded from the game came from getting better at it and chasing your own high score. I wanted to cultivate that feeling, so I explored other approaches; still haven't nailed it yet, though.

Perseverance

I've made a few games before, but this is the one I've polished this much and will become my first truly published game. It's exciting, but I don't want to burn out of this project, so I've been trying to have fun while working on it.

I worked on little delight-minded features that weren't really critical to the M.V.P. Things like doodling pixel art I didn't need yet, sourcing music, the crt_filter, intro splashes, music banner, etc. In the next section, you might clock that I'm currently working on another "fun exploration" rather than "critical" game polish. Still, I'm happy with the direction I'm going.

Before I jump into that, I want to shout out the artists I sourced the music from. I found Adya Nadira and Miguel Angel (miguelangell960) on Fiverr, and they created some wonderful tracks that were a big hit with the internal testers. I honestly think their tracks were doing a lot of heavy lifting for the polished feeling of the game, so that inspired me to make the little music banner that shows up when the track switches. I wanted to highlight the tracks, and honestly that banner itself made the game look more polished.

Despite my best efforts, I eventually felt stuck on the project so I took a break for a bit. Last major thing I did was get feature parity on the android version, and figure out a (very manual) build and deploy pipeline for android. I doodled some pixel art here and there, but didn't really make any headway in the project. I focused more on work and my personal-life tasks.

The Tense Present

We're so back

But that break proved to be not too long! I've been ruminating on how to make my game more fun. Selfishly, I wanted to have more fun playing my own game, so I tried to explore that. To be clear I know it's unwise to focus on yourself as the player as a game dev, because it's really hard to think objectively as a player when you're the developer. However, I think it'll be easier for me to make a fun game if I can also play it endlessly through emergent gameplay or have a "list of things to unlock". So I'm adapting roguelike elements in this game (sorry, if you're tired of them).

I actually jumpstarted this effort by reworking my personal website. You're probably reading from my website right now, unless I started posting on Substack. It was a bit of a roundabout way, but I reworked my website so I could highlight Spilly Goose. Ever since, I've been on a motivated kick to keep working on the game, that's what led me to release 1.0.9. I'm still feeling the rush of working on this, hence the name of this section.

1.0.9 New features

  • Cafe Dialogue/Gameplay
    • Created a dialogue system
    • Added an intro cutscene and some pre/post run dialogue. For now, the cafe is just dialogue interaction tail-ending your runs.
    • "Why is the goose spilling" was a frequently asked question and I wanted to answer it in this fun way.
    • This will serve the gameplay loop soon, trust
    • Also added scene transitions
  • Save System
    • This isn't going to be apparent to the players, it's more of an internal thing.
    • Since there's more dialogue and player interactions to save, I decided it was time to have a proper save system.
